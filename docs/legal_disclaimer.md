# Legal Disclaimer

## Nature of this tool

The AI Act Risk Classifier is an **experimental research tool** designed to assist in the preliminary analysis of AI systems under Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (the EU AI Act). It is intended for educational and informational purposes only.

## What this tool is NOT

- **Not legal advice.** Output generated by this tool does not constitute legal advice, legal opinion, or a formal compliance assessment.
- **Not a substitute for professional judgment.** The EU AI Act requires case-by-case assessment that takes into account the specific technical implementation, deployment context, and organisational circumstances of each AI system.
- **Not authoritative.** Only the Official Journal of the European Union (OJ EU) text of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 is legally binding. This tool's outputs are approximations.

## Known limitations

1. **Corpus coverage**: The classifier operates on a subset of the AI Act text. Provisions outside the core risk classification articles (Articles 5, 6, 50; Annexes I, II, III) are not fully represented.

2. **Ambiguous cases**: The AI Act contains numerous concepts that require authoritative interpretation (e.g., "general purpose AI", "safety component", "substantial modification"). This tool flags ambiguities but cannot resolve them.

3. **Evolving guidance**: The European AI Office and national supervisory authorities will issue guidelines, standards, and decisions that affect the interpretation of the regulation. This tool reflects the text as of its training corpus date.

4. **Language limitations**: Classification quality may vary between the Spanish and English corpus versions due to translation differences in legal terminology.

5. **No sector-specific analysis**: Some sectors (medical devices, aviation, financial services) have additional applicable frameworks that interact with the AI Act. This tool does not perform cross-regulatory analysis.

## Appropriate use

This tool is appropriate for:
- Initial screening to identify whether a use case warrants deeper legal analysis
- Educational exploration of AI Act classification logic
- Portfolio and demonstration purposes

This tool is **not** appropriate for:
- Making compliance decisions without qualified legal review
- Regulatory filings or conformity assessments
- Advising clients on their AI Act obligations

## Who to consult

For formal AI Act compliance analysis, consult a qualified legal professional with expertise in EU AI regulation and, where applicable, sector-specific regulatory counsel.
